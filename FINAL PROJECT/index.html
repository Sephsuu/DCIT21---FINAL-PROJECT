About Us

    The Philippine National Red Cross (PNRC) is the national society of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement in the Philippines. An independent, humanitarian organization that provides assistance to people affected by natural disasters, armed conflicts, and other emergencies. The PNRC is guided by the seven Fundamental Principles of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement: humanity, impartiality, neutrality, independence, voluntary service, unity and universality. PNRC provides a range of services including emergency medical services, blood donation, disaster risk reduction and management, health and care services, social services, and training. They respond to natural and man-made disasters, like typhoons, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions and armed conflicts, among others.

Life Support Training

Among PRC’s specializations is providing professional first aid and safety training for corporations which can be customized to meet the client’s work nature and requirements. Knowledge in first aid will enable individuals to assist people who are injured in the event of an emergency situation or accident in the workplace.

Philippine Red Cross

Born officially in 1947, but with roots that traces back to the revolutionary days, the Philippine Red Cross has truly become the premier humanitarian organization in the country, committed to provide quality life-saving services that protect the life and dignity especially of indigent Filipinos in vulnerable situations.

Supporting the Philippine Red Cross is a way to invest in the well-being of the community and contribute to a more resilient and sustainable future for all Filipinos.
